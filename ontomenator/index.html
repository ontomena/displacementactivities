philosophy / ontology
Aletheia
unconcealment — truth as disclosure

Heidegger was obsessed with how Greek, and to some extent German, preserved verbal and processual senses in words that later Latin translation and modern European languages flattened into static, thing-like nouns. His key example is the Greek word ἀλήθεια (aletheia) — usually translated as "truth," but Heidegger insists it means "un-concealment" or "un-hiddenness," preserving an active, event-like quality: truth as something that happens, a wresting-from-concealment, not a fixed property of propositions. He argued that the Latin translation of Greek philosophical vocabulary was a kind of catastrophe — energeia becoming actualitas, ousia becoming substantia, physis becoming natura — each time replacing a word with a living verbal core (doing, presencing, emerging) with something more inert and object-like. He saw this as the beginning of metaphysics forgetting the question of Being.

A broader linguistic observation goes beyond Heidegger into typological differences between languages and how they handle the noun/verb boundary: Several indigenous American languages, particularly Nootka (Nuu-chah-nulth) and some Algonquian languages, were famously discussed by Benjamin Lee Whorf as being far more "verb-heavy" — where what English would express as a noun ("house," "flame") is expressed as a process or event. Whorf's specific claims about Hopi have been disputed, but the broader typological point about languages varying in how rigidly they partition nouns from verbs holds up.

Japanese has a very fluid relationship between verbs and nouns that resists the kind of hard substance-ontology that Indo-European noun structures encourage. For example, 1. doing noun+verb fusion, e.g. kopī suru: kopī (copy, as noun) + suru (do) = make a copy. Also 2. verb+noun fusion, e.g. wasuremono: (wasure (forget) + mono (thing).

Heidegger was basically making the argument developed by Sapir-Whorf, but specifically about Greek versus Latin, and by extension the whole Western tradition.

Being and Time §7: the original Greek sense of phainomenon is self-showing — things coming forth into presence from themselves.

The Latin translation of Greek philosophy as catastrophe: energeia → actualitas, ousia → substantia, physis → natura. Each replacing a living verbal core with something inert.

Aletheia: truth as un-concealment, an event — not a fixed property of propositions. Truth happens.

Languages that preserve verbal, processual senses vs those that flatten into static nouns. Sapir-Whorf applied to metaphysics.
